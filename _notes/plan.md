# Freelance Website Plan — dustinwoodard.net

## 1) Goal of the Website

**Primary Objective:**
Convert visitors into leads (messages, calls, or form submissions)

**Secondary Objectives:**
- Showcase your work (portfolio/demo)
- Establish credibility and professionalism
- Clearly communicate your pricing and services

---

## 2) Site Structure (Simple + Conversion Focused)

### Pages

1. **Home (Landing Page)** ← most important
2. **Contact Page**
3. *(Optional later)* Portfolio / Case Studies

---

## 3) Homepage Layout (High-Converting Structure)

### 1. Hero Section (Above the Fold)

**Goal:** Immediately communicate value

- Headline:
  > “Custom High-Performance Websites That Drive Results”

- Subheadline:
  > “I design and build fast, modern websites for businesses that want more leads and better performance.”

- Call-to-Action (CTA):
  - “Get a Quote”
  - “Start a Project”

- Optional:
  - Short credibility line:
    > “Fast delivery • SEO-ready • Built to convert”

---

### 2. Services Section

**Goal:** Show what you actually do

- Custom Website Development
- Landing Pages
- Website Redesigns
- Performance Optimization

Keep descriptions short and outcome-focused.

---

### 3. Pricing Section (Key for Filtering Clients)

Display your tiers clearly:

#### Starter

- $2,500+
- Small business sites
- 4–6 pages

#### Growth (Recommended)

- $5,000+
- Conversion-focused builds
- CMS + performance optimization

#### Custom / Advanced

- $8,000+
- Advanced features, integrations, custom systems

**CTA under pricing:**
→ “Request a Custom Quote”

---

### 4. Portfolio / Demo Section

**Goal:** Show proof

- 2–4 strong projects
- Use:
  - Screenshots
  - Short descriptions
  - “What problem it solves”

If no clients yet:
→ Use demo projects (this is fine)

---

### 5. Process Section (Build Trust)

Simple 4–5 steps:

1. Discovery
2. Strategy
3. Design
4. Development
5. Launch

---

### 6. Final CTA Section

Strong close:

> “Ready to build something that actually performs?”

Buttons:
- “Start a Project”
- “Contact Me”

---

## 4) Contact Page

### Keep it simple

**Form Fields:**
- Name
- Email
- Project description
- Budget range (important for filtering)
- Timeline

**Optional:**
- Calendly link (for booking calls)

---

## 5) Design Direction

**Style:**
- Clean, modern, minimal
- Dark or light theme (pick one and stay consistent)
- Strong typography
- Lots of spacing

**Tech Stack Suggestion:**
- Laravel + Inertia.js
- Mysql
- Tailwind CSS
- Hosted on Digital Ocean

---

## 6) Pricing Your Own Site (What You Should Charge for This)

This is your **baseline product offer**.

### If you sold this exact site to a client

- Landing page: **$1,500 – $3,000**
- Contact page: **$200 – $500**

**Total Package Value:**
> **$2,000 – $3,500**

---

## 7) Your Actual Cost to Build It

- Hosting: ~$0–$10/month
- Domain: ~$10/year
- Email (optional): ~$0–$6/month

**Total cost: very low → high ROI asset**

---

## 8) Messaging (Important)

Everything should communicate:

- Speed
- Performance
- Results
- Professionalism

Avoid:
- Overly technical language
- Long paragraphs

Focus on:
> “What the client gets”

---

## 9) Build Plan (Execution Steps)

### Step 1

- Buy domain: **dustinwoodard.net**

### Step 2

- Set up project (Next.js + Tailwind)

### Step 3

- Build homepage sections in this order:
  1. Hero
  2. Services
  3. Pricing
  4. Portfolio
  5. Process
  6. CTA

### Step 4

- Build contact page + form handling

### Step 5

- Deploy (Vercel)

---

## 10) MVP Scope (Don’t Overbuild)

Launch with:
- 1 strong homepage
- 1 contact page
- 2–3 portfolio demos

That’s enough to start getting clients.

---

## Key Insight

This site is not a “portfolio.”

It is a:
> **sales tool designed to generate $5k+ projects**

Keep it simple, direct, and focused on conversion.
